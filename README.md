## SLIDE 9: Trust & Safety — Customer Protection

This is where a lot of marketplace projects fall apart, so let's address it head-on.

Product quality issues: if a customer receives a defective or damaged product, they have a 30-day return window. The seller covers return shipping. Full refund or replacement. And our AI flags sellers with high defect rates so we can take action before it becomes a pattern.

Payment disputes: Motion mediates through the admin panel. Seller funds are held in escrow until the dispute is resolved. The customer is always protected first. Always.

Delivery issues: every shipment requires tracking. If a seller fails to deliver, Motion compensates the customer, then we recover the cost from the seller's account balance. The customer never takes the hit.

And here's the big one: Motion-backed refund policy. Even if the seller disappears, Motion covers the refund. Yes, that's an operational expense. But it builds trust, and trust is how we win long-term.

We're targeting less than 2% defect rate, which matches Amazon's standard. Twenty-four hour max response time for disputes. One hundred percent customer refund guarantee, backed by Motion.

This isn't just a marketplace. It's a Motion-quality marketplace.

## SLIDE 10: Business Model — Triple Revenue Stream

Let's talk money. We have three distinct revenue streams working together.

First: commission on sales. We take 8 to 15% on marketplace GMV. For context, Amazon takes 15 to 20%, so our lower fees make us more attractive to quality sellers.

Second: seller subscriptions. Basic is free to get them started. Standard is $99 a month. Premium is $299 a month. Each tier unlocks better visibility and analytics. This is recurring revenue from active sellers.

Third: featured listings. Sellers pay to promote their products in search results. This is an additional revenue layer on top of commissions and subscriptions.

Now let's break down the Year 1 projection: $740,000 in ARR. Here's where that comes from.

We're assuming 200 sellers generating $5 million in GMV. That's being conservative.

Commission revenue: about $500,000. That's a 10% average commission rate on $5 million in GMV.

Subscription revenue: about $180,000. That's a mix of Standard and Premium tiers across our 200 sellers.

Featured listings: about $60,000. We're assuming roughly 30% of sellers will pay for promotion.

Add it up: $740,000 in Year 1. And if we execute well, we'll beat that number.

## SLIDE 11: Why This Doesn't Cannibalize Motion Sales

Let's make this crystal clear, because I know the question is coming.

The ranking is honest. It's based on true relevance. But Motion gets priority in tie-breaks, and the distribution is tendenciada 55/45 in our favor. That means in a blended result set, Motion products will make up about 55% of what the customer sees, marketplace about 45%.

Today, when we don't have a product, those customers leave. They're the high-intent search users — remember, they convert at two to three times the rate of browsers. They go to Grainger or Amazon, and we get zero. Zero revenue. Zero relationship.

With the marketplace, we retain them. We earn commission. We keep the relationship. And we cross-sell Motion products in the process.

Fifty-five percent Motion in blended results. Forty-five percent marketplace, filling the gaps. One hundred percent customer retention — they never leave the Motion ecosystem.

This doesn't hurt Motion. It protects Motion.

## SLIDE 14: Go-To-Market Plan

We have a functional MVP. Now let's talk about how we actually launch this thing and make it real.

Phase one: seller kickstart. Week 1 to 2.

First move: leverage Mirakl's network of over 100,000 sellers. These are sellers already active on the Mirakl platform. They know how marketplace platforms work. They're looking for new channels. We contact them directly.

Second: industrial seller outreach. We've identified 50 verified sellers in Motion's niche — companies selling bearings, motors, fasteners, electrical components. We pitch them lower fees: 8 to 15% versus Amazon's 15 to 20%. That's the hook.

Second: internal rollout to Motion's sales team. We equip our reps with the marketplace as a solution for "we don't carry that" objections. Instead of losing the customer, they redirect to marketplace and we earn commission.

Third: email campaign to active buyers. Subject line: "Motion just got 10x bigger." We showcase new product categories now available through the marketplace.

Fourth: we track everything. Conversion rate. Basket size increase. Seller performance. Customer satisfaction. We iterate based on real data.

Success metrics for the first 90 days: 50-plus active sellers on the platform. $250,000 in marketplace GMV. Fifteen percent or higher basket size lift. And searches with results doubling — meaning we're actually solving the empty search problem.
